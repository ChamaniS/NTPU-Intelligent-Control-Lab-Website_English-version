INTELLIGENT MODELING AND CONTROL LAB

Address: Room 607, Elytone EECS Building

Phone: (02)8674-1111 ext: 68839

The Intelligent Modeling and Control Lab was originally created to look for a softer, more user-friendly interface in the experience of human and machine system operations, citing computational methods as tools for intelligent decision making. Furthermore, it explores the problems of various human-machine interaction systems. It is expected to find the criteria for the behavior between system input and output in a large number of data in a human-computer interaction system, model it, and then use control theory and system engineering to establish prediction and control. mechanism. Its application is mainly in the detection, modeling, and control of mobile behaviors such as human body and implements with behavioral capabilities. The laboratory focuses on the intelligent modeling of kinematics or dynamics of behavioral objects, such as robots, biomechanics, and machines with frequent human-computer interaction. The integration of its overall system is also included in the research direction of interest in this laboratory.

RESEARCH DIRECTION

A. Kinematics, Dynamics & Motion Sensors

The emerging development of the motion sensors, such as accelerometers, gyroscopes, and the other miniature devices, are very suitable to develop some helpful mobility-aided stuffs. To achieve a good mobility or motion of a mechanism, especially, that for biomedical use, the topic often corresponds to the analysis of its kinematics and dynamics. It is hence the elementarily importance to model the kinematics and dynamics of the stuffs. The learning scheme can be a good candidate for the modeling. By facilitating the technique, we try to pave a general way to reach the goal.

B. Intelligent Modeling and Control of Motion Interactive Robots

To interact with human beings, computational intelligence and control strategies are introduced to produce a safe and graceful motion. By facilitating modeling techniques to study the behaviors of research target, an inverse problem of the target is established and conduct an adequate corresponding control. In the particular topic, we focus first the motion control of the kind of robots who would interact with human beings.

C. Dynamic Path Planning & Tracking to Interact Human Beings' Environments

Due to the developments of computational intelligence and control technique, the path planning and navigation of ground mobile agent are potential for a highly dynamic environment. The research integrates emerging engineering facilities and techniques to plan and follow a robotic/vehicular movement to interact with human beings for applications in such as hospitals, shopping malls, or libraries.

D. Machine Learning & Support Vector Machines

The problematic differences drift the techniques developed in machine learning. It lightens up the room for developing varieties of the techniques. Intrinsically, the developments firmly rooted to the basic rules of machine learning could be more meaningful and sustainable. From the aspect, a broad survey of related topics and in-depth consideration of the facts behind a problem sometimes conduct a significant innovation. In the topic, we facilitating support vector machine as a paradigm for such an inspection. With much grounded inspiration, solutions strongly relevant to its problem can be addressed and be followed up.
